About TytlLab

Built from twenty years inside the closing process.

TytlLab wasn't designed in a conference room. The people who built it spent two decades on every side of the real estate transaction and got tired of watching the same coordination failures cost time, money, and deals. This is what they built to fix it.

The origin

The process wasn't broken. The infrastructure was.

The founders of TytlLab came to title during the days when they didn't have the aid of computers. Their path ran from construction management through residential brokerage, through dozens of deals as developers and agents in New York City. At every stage, the closing process was the same: capable people forced into low-value coordination work because there was no system built for them.

Files stalled waiting on vendor replies, exceptions were tracked in spreadsheets, and closers spent mornings sending follow-up emails instead of working files.

TytlLab is the software they built to manage and resolve these issues by employing agentic AI to handle coordination, follow-ups, and tracking across every file at scale.

The product

What two decades of operational experience produces.

Seven AI assistants, one file.

Each assistant owns a phase of the file: intake, search coordination, clearance, closing prep, recording, and policy. They work in sequence, hand off cleanly, and flag anything that needs a human.

Every open file on one screen.

The TytlLab dashboard shows the status of every file in your office in real time. If something needs attention, it surfaces automatically.

More volume. Same team.

Title agencies using TytlLab don't hire more coordinators when they grow. They let the agents absorb the coordination load while their closers focus on the work that requires judgment.

The platform

TytlLab is part of something larger.

TytlLab is built by SCL Holdings — a company building the connected infrastructure for the real estate transaction, from search to close to record. TytlLab covers the title agency. The rest covers the deal.
